№ 1

Cinnamon Morning Water

for the belly that wakes up bloated
  • 1 cinnamon stick (or 1 teaspoon ground)
  • 2 cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon raw honey
  • A small squeeze of lemon

Drop the cinnamon stick into a small pot of water. Let it simmer gently for ten minutes — never boil it hard. Take it off the heat, stir in the honey and lemon, and pour it into your cup.

Drink it slowly, first thing in the morning, before anything else.

My mother used to say cinnamon "wakes the belly up gently." I never understood what she meant until I started doing this myself.

№ 2

Fennel & Ginger Tea

for after-supper bloat and a heavy stomach
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • A thumb of fresh ginger, sliced thin
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon honey (optional)

Put the fennel seeds and ginger slices into your kettle with the water. Bring it to a boil, then turn it down and let it simmer for eight minutes. Strain it into a mug.

Drink it warm, half an hour after supper, when the heaviness sits in your stomach.

Fennel breaks up trapped wind, and ginger pulls the heat out of inflammation. Together they're the gentlest digestion remedy I know.

№ 3

Apple Cider & Lemon Reset

for swollen ankles and water you can't shake
  • 1 tablespoon raw apple cider vinegar (with the "mother")
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 1 teaspoon raw honey
  • 1 cup warm water

Stir everything together in a glass until the honey dissolves. Drink it slowly through a straw if your teeth are sensitive the vinegar can be sharp.

Every morning before anything else touches your lips. Don't skip the lemon it's what makes the vinegar gentle on your stomach.

By the third morning, the marks your socks leave on your ankles by evening should start to disappear.

№ 4

Golden Turmeric Milk

for stiff joints and restless sleep
  • 1 cup whole milk (or oat milk)
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • A pinch of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon raw honey

Warm the milk in a small pan until you see steam rising never let it boil. Whisk in the turmeric and pepper. Pour it into your mug and stir in the honey at the end so the heat doesn't kill it.

Drink it warm, an hour before bed.

The black pepper is not optional. Without it, your body can't use the turmeric. Our grandmothers knew this long before doctors confirmed it.

№ 5

Bay Leaf Calming Tea

for stress that holds onto your belly
  • 3 dried bay leaves
  • 2 cups water
  • A small piece of cinnamon stick
  • Honey to taste

Bring the water to a soft boil. Drop in the bay leaves and the cinnamon stick. Cover the pot and let it steep on low heat for fifteen minutes. The steam holds the medicine don't lift the lid.

Sip it slowly in the late afternoon, when the day's worries start sitting heavy on you.

When stress builds up, your body holds onto fat the way it holds onto its breath. This tea teaches it to let go.

№ 6

Lemon & Parsley Liver Flush

for the heaviness that sits behind your right ribs
  • A small handful of fresh parsley
  • 1 whole lemon, peel and all
  • 1 cup of water
  • A pinch of sea salt

Wash the parsley well. Cut the lemon into chunks and toss it all into a blender with the water and salt. Blend until smooth, then strain the pulp out through a cheesecloth or fine sieve.

Drink it once a week, in the morning, on an empty stomach. Don't do it more often your liver needs gentleness, not punishment.

If you've been carrying too many years of fried food and processed meat, this is the recipe that opens the gates again.
